06 METHODOLOGY

NIA Interventions Testing Program (ITP)

The ITP is a peer-reviewed, NIA-funded program that tests compounds for lifespan extension in genetically heterogeneous (UM-HET3) mice across three independent sites (Jackson Laboratory, University of Michigan, University of Texas Health San Antonio). It is the gold standard for mammalian lifespan studies because of its rigorous multi-site design, large sample sizes (~160 mice per group per sex), and use of genetically diverse mice that avoid strain-specific artifacts.

Composite Score

Each compound receives a 0–100 composite score weighting: ITP result status and effect size (40%), reproducibility and both-sex benefit (20%), human trial count and phase advancement (25%), and mechanistic plausibility and safety profile (15%). Scores are relative rankings to highlight the most promising translational candidates.

Human Trial Data

Human trial counts and phase data are sourced from ClinicalTrials.gov, filtered for aging, longevity, healthspan, and age-related disease endpoints. Trial phases reflect the most advanced registered trial for each compound in an aging-relevant context.
